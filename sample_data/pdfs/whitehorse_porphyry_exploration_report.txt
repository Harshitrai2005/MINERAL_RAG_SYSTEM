GEOLOGICAL EXPLORATION REPORT
Whitehorse Porphyry Copper-Gold Deposit

Executive Summary

The Whitehorse prospect represents a mid-stage porphyry Cu-Au system with
multiple alteration zones and significant geochemical anomalies. Preliminary
drilling to 40 meters depth has intersected potassic and phyllic alteration
zones with elevated copper (up to 3,456 ppm) and gold (up to 1.56 ppm) grades.

Regional Geology

The Whitehorse property is located within the Tertiary porphyry belt,
characterized by calc-alkaline granitic intrusions of Eocene age. Host
rocks include Cretaceous meta-sediments and Paleozoic schists intruded
by multiple phases of granodiorite and granite porphyries.

Mineralization

Four main alteration zones have been defined:

1. Oxide Zone (0-15m depth)
   - Propylitic alteration (chlorite, epidote, calcite)
   - Low Cu-Au grades (Cu: 45-95 ppm; Au: 0.01-0.05 ppm)
   - Iron oxide staining visible in hand specimen

2. Transition Zone (15-25m depth)
   - Phyllic alteration (sericite, quartz, pyrite)
   - Elevated Cu-Au grades (Cu: 145-325 ppm; Au: 0.08-0.18 ppm)
   - Moderate disseminated sulfides

3. Primary Zone (25-40m depth)
   - Potassic alteration (feldspar + biotite + magnetite)
   - High Cu-Au grades (Cu: 1,050-3,456 ppm; Au: 0.68-1.56 ppm)
   - Dense disseminated to stockwork chalcopyrite, bornite

4. Deep Primary (>40m depth)
   - Phyllic transitional (quartz + sericite)
   - Moderate grades, approaching potassic core

Geochemical Results

Soil geochemical survey of 1,000 samples across 8 km2 defined an anomalous Cu
halo (>300 ppm) covering 2.5 km2 and centered on drill area. Au soil values
reached 125 ppb in peak area. Mo, As, Sb accompany Cu-Au anomalies,
characteristic of porphyry systems. Background Cu is 32 ppm, Au is 6 ppb.

Structural Geology

The deposit occurs at the intersection of two regional fault sets:
- NW-trending fault (steep, dextral sense of movement)
- ENE-trending fault (moderate dip, sinistral sense of movement)

This intersection appears to have focused intrusion and mineralization,
creating a structural trap for fluid flow and ore deposition.

Resource Estimation

Preliminary inferred resource (kriged from 4 drill holes):
- Volume: 85 million tonnes
- Average Cu grade: 0.35% (0.25-0.45% credible interval)
- Average Au grade: 0.28 g/t (0.18-0.38 g/t credible interval)
- Contained Cu: 295,000 tonnes
- Contained Au: 24,000 kg (770,000 oz)

Recommendations

1. Extend drill grid to define strike and dip of mineralization
2. Acquire deep IP/resistivity survey to test for deeper potassic cores
3. Geological mapping to trace fault controls at surface
4. Bulk sample from potassic zone for metallurgical testing
5. Stream sediment survey in peripheral drainages to detect hidden extensions

Conclusion

Whitehorse shows all hallmarks of a significant Cu-Au porphyry system.
The intersection of regional structures, presence of high-grade zones at
shallow depth, and extensive surface and subsurface geochemical anomalies
warrant progression to pre-feasibility stage exploration.

GEOLOGICAL SURVEY REPORT
Mount Centauri Porphyry Project — Phase II Exploration
Report Number: GEO-2024-042
Prepared by: Sierra Exploration Ltd.
Date: June 2024

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Mount Centauri project covers 2,450 hectares in the Central Andes at elevations between 1,200 and 2,100 metres. Phase II exploration has delineated a significant porphyry copper-gold system with an associated epithermal overprint. An inferred mineral resource of 185 Mt grading 0.42 g/t Au and 0.38% Cu (AuEq cutoff 0.2 g/t) has been estimated, containing approximately 2.5 Moz Au and 703 kt Cu. The project warrants advancement to pre-feasibility.

GEOLOGICAL SETTING

Regional Geology
The project area lies within the Miocene porphyry copper belt of the Central Andes. Host rocks comprise a calc-alkaline magmatic arc sequence including andesites, dacites, rhyolites, and associated intrusive rocks of granodiorite and monzonite composition. The intrusive complex is dated at 12-14 Ma (Miocene) by Re-Os molybdenite geochronology and is part of the regionally extensive Centauri Magmatic Belt.

Structural Geology
The main structural control is a NNW-trending fault corridor that has channelled hydrothermal fluids and controlled the geometry of the mineralised system. The Central Potassic Core strikes 045° and dips steeply (78°) to the northwest. Secondary NE-trending tensional fractures host quartz-sulphide veins with elevated Au-Ag grades.

MINERALIZATION

Porphyry System
The central potassic core (Zone MZ-001) hosts the main economic mineralisation. Chalcopyrite, bornite, and molybdenite occur as disseminations and A-type veins within a K-silicate alteration envelope (secondary biotite-K-feldspar-magnetite). Gold occurs as inclusions within chalcopyrite and as free gold in quartz veins. Average grades within the resource shell are 2.85 g/t Au and 0.39% Cu. The highest grades (up to 6.89 g/t Au, 0.72% Cu) occur in the deepest drill intercepts, indicating the system is open at depth.

Epithermal Overprint
The Northwest Epithermal Corridor (Zone MZ-002) represents a structurally controlled high-sulphidation epithermal system overprinting the upper parts of the porphyry. Silicic and advanced argillic alteration (alunite-kaolinite-pyrophyllite) characterises this zone. Electrum, argentite, and arsenopyrite are the principal ore minerals. Pathfinder elements As-Sb-Te define a 400m strike-length anomaly. Grades reach 12.4 g/t Au and 92.5 g/t Ag.

Alteration Zoning
Classic porphyry alteration zoning is preserved:
- Potassic core: biotite + K-feldspar + magnetite + chalcopyrite + bornite
- Phyllic intermediate: sericite + pyrite + quartz + chalcopyrite (Zone MZ-003)
- Argillic outer: kaolinite + smectite + pyrite
- Propylitic peripheral: chlorite + epidote + carbonate

GEOCHEMISTRY

Soil and Rock Geochemistry
A 200m × 50m soil geochemical survey covering the project area returned a coherent multi-element anomaly of Cu > 200 ppm, Au > 0.05 ppm, and Mo > 10 ppm extending over a 1.2 km × 0.8 km area. The anomaly is centred on the Central Potassic Core and is open to the north.

Lithogeochemistry
Whole-rock geochemical data indicate the mineralised intrusions are calc-alkaline, high-K dacite-rhyodacite in composition, typical of fertile porphyry systems. Elevated Sr/Y ratios (>50) and La/Yb values (>30) suggest an adakitic affinity, a geochemical signature increasingly recognised as correlating with Cu-Au-rich porphyry systems globally.

DRILLING RESULTS

Summary of Diamond Drilling
Phase II drilling comprised 28 diamond drill holes totalling 8,450 metres. Significant intercepts include:

DH-001: 25.0m at 2.34 g/t Au, 0.28% Cu from 20m depth
DH-001: 30.0m at 5.12 g/t Au, 0.42% Cu from 25m depth (best intercept)
DH-002: 20.0m at 6.89 g/t Au, 0.72% Cu from 25m depth
DH-003: 18.5m at 1.48 g/t Au, 0.25% Cu from 20m depth

True widths are estimated at 75-85% of drill intercept widths based on structural measurements.

RESOURCE ESTIMATE

An inferred resource estimate has been prepared using ordinary kriging interpolation of composite assay data at 5m intervals:

Classification: Inferred Mineral Resource
Tonnes: 185 Mt
Au grade: 0.42 g/t
Cu grade: 0.38%
Contained Au: 2,500 koz (2.5 Moz)
Contained Cu: 703 kt
Cut-off grade: 0.20 g/t AuEq
Density: 2.65 t/m³

RECOMMENDATIONS

Based on Phase II results, Sierra Exploration recommends the following work program for Phase III:

1. Priority: Infill diamond drilling at 50m spacing within the Central Potassic Core (budget: USD 4.2M, 12,000m, 40 holes). The resource remains open at depth and along the northern plunge.

2. High Priority: Step-out drilling to test the northern extension of Zone MZ-002 along the NW epithermal corridor (budget: USD 1.8M, 5,000m, 15 holes).

3. Medium Priority: Geophysical surveys (IP-resistivity and CSAMT) to map the deep porphyry geometry and identify additional drill targets beneath the potassic core (budget: USD 0.6M).

4. Metallurgical testwork: Preliminary bottle-roll cyanide leach tests and flotation characterisation on composite samples from the main resource zones (budget: USD 0.4M).

Total recommended Phase III budget: USD 7.0 million
Timeline: 18-24 months to pre-feasibility level resource

CONCLUSIONS

Mount Centauri exhibits all characteristics of a world-class porphyry Cu-Au deposit. The discovery of high-grade epithermal Au-Ag mineralisation overprinting the porphyry system represents an additional economic opportunity not fully captured in the current resource estimate. Continued investment is strongly warranted.
